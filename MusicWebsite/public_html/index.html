JAZZ

Brief history of Jazz

Jazz first began in African-American communities in the US. It appeared in the late 19th and early 20th century. A big part of jazz is actually improvisation, which shows the rich feelings behind artists of this genre. Most performances of jazz are actually artists making up songs on the stage. At first, jazz was a genre mainly known to get people dancing, after the first few years, the popularity of jazz grew rapidly. Although jazz grew in the United States, it didn’t stop there. Jazz spread to many countries all over the world, some even say that jazz is “one of the United States’ greatest exports to the world”.

Famous artists

  • Louis Armstrong
  • Duke Ellington
  • Benny Carter
  • Frank Sinatra
  • Mary Lou Williams
  • Nat King Cole
  • Ella Fitzgerald
  • Oscar Peterson

Duke Ellington

Duke Ellington is a bandleader and also a composer of jazz music. He was one of the greatest musical jazz artists of the 20th century, who was well recognised as a composer, bandleader and instrumentalist.

Frank Sinatra

Sinatra is a famous jazz and pop artist, as well as being a famous actor. He is often called the greatest American singer of all time. He is one of the best-selling artists of all time. More than 150 million of his records have been sold all over the world.

Louis Armstrong

Armstrong was born in 1901 in Louisana, United States. He was a leading trumpeter and is a very famous jazz artist. His song ‘What a wonderful World’ is known worldwide. Armstrong would play in marching bands and would play in Mississippi riverboat dance bands.

Classical

Brief history of Classical

The genre classical is one of the oldest music genres in the world. The earliest pieces of classical music were composed even as early as the 12th century. The second half of the 16th century show the beginning of tradition which gave the ‘common’ classical music that we hear today. At the very end of the 16th century, opera and orchestra came into the picture. The biggest ‘issue’ for classical music appeared in the early 19th century, when during the Great War, composers varied their styles of classical music.

Famous artists

  • Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart
  • Ludwig van Beethoven
  • Johann Christian Bach
  • Frederic Chopin
  • Johannes Brahms
  • Antonio Vivaldi
  • Franz Schubert
  • Robert Schumann

Wolgang Amadeus Mozart

Wolgang Amadeus Mozart was born in Salzburg in 1756. He was a well known child prodigy. He did many tours through Europe. By the age of 16 he had already done concerts in over 10 countries. The last opera that he composed was called ‘The Magic Flute’, it was composed 3 months before his death.

Ludwig van Beethoven

Ludwig van Beethoven was born in Germany. He was a famous pianist and composer. He is most famous for being famous, and yet still one of the best composers of his time. Beethoven died in 1827 in Vienna.

Frederic Chopin

Frederic Chopin was one of the best polish composers of all time. He was a Polish French composer of the Romantic period. Chopin spent a lot of his time in Vienna to gain music experience. As well as being an exceptional composer, Chopin was also a pianist, who wrote many piano pieces and concerts.

Reggae

Brief history of Reggae

The first origins of Reggae come from Jamaica, in the late 1960’s. They soon became the main music of the country. After only 5/6 years, this music genre became famous in Britain, the United States and Africa. The band that really brought popularity to this genre was ‘The Wailers’. The word reggae came from a phrase they had in Jamaica ‘rege-rege’ which can be translated as ‘rags or ragged clothing’. Reggae had a big impact on the Jamaican culture, it was a way of artists being able to get their word spoken and heard.

Famous artists

  • Bob Marley
  • Peter Tosh
  • Burning Spear
  • Ruth Brown
  • Magic!
  • Buju Banton
  • Shaggy
  • Sean Paul

Jimmy Cliff

Jimmy Cliff was the biggest influencer of reggae music. His first album was The Harder They Come, published in 1972. Jimmy was born in Jamaica, and even in childhood his talent as a musican was discovered.

Bob Marley

Full name Robert Nesta Marley, was a Jamaican singer who wrote his own songs. He is the most famous Reggae artist of all time. Bob Marley also wrote Christian music in his later years.

Peter Tosh

Peter Tosh is a well known Reggae artist. He was a singer, composer and he is also known as a rebel. He used to tour with The Rolling Stones, and he would collaborate with other famous musicians such as Mick Jagger.

Country

Brief history of Country

Country music was first established in the 1910s. It first appeared in the southern United States. The roots of country music came from folk music and blues. It was mainly played on instruments such as an acoustic guitar, mandolin, fiddle and banjo. One of the first, most famous, country artists was Jimmie Rodgers and the Carter family. The music genre grew, as families would sit around the house listening to radios. This music became especially popular and people assosciated the genre with cowboys, which still is a prejudice to this day.

Famous artists

  • Carrie Underwood
  • Dolly Parton
  • Tim McGraw
  • Johnny Cash
  • John Denver
  • Dolly Parton
  • Taylor Swift
  • Hank Williams

Dolly Parton

Dolly Parton is an American singer, songwriter, actress, businesswoman and author. She is most famous for her country music. Her most well known song being “Jolene”. Parton is one of the most famous female country performers of all time. She has won many awards, including Grammy Awards, Academy Awards, American Music Awards and many more.

Johnny Cash

Johnny Cash was a part rock, part country artist, most famous as the essence of country music. He was a rising artist after World War II. In a way, many people say that Johnny Cash created his own subgenre of country music. To this day he is the most beloved artist by many.

Carrie Underwood

Carrie Underwood is an American actress and singer. She became famous in 2005 when she won American Idol. Her most famous country music single was “Inside Your Heaven”, which was also the first country single to make it on the US Billboard Hot 100. Carrie Underwood has sold more than 65 million records worldwide.

Pop

Brief history of Pop

Early pop artists include Frank Sinatra, Bobby Darin and Elvis Presley. In the 1960s, The Beatles appeared, bringing even more popularity to pop music. The 1970s were almost like a ‘disco era’ for pop music. Artists such as ABBA, The Rolling Stones and Stevie Wonder appeared, artists that are still well known even to this day. 1980s and 1990s brought artists like Michael Jackson and Mariah Carey, Elton John, Selena and many more. The name ‘pop’ music, is actually an abbreviation of the word “popular”. Pop music is mostly described as people-friendly, memorable and relatable, with usually a very catchy chorus. The goal of pop music is to visually present the sound. Pretty much all instruments are used in this genre of music, but the most commonly used are guitars, piano and drums.

Metal

Brief history of Metal

The first Heavy Metal band was offically Black Sabbath. It first appeared in the late 1960s. The instrument used most in heavy metal music is the electric guitar. Metal first appeared in the United Kingdom, in a city called Birmingham. It appeared as after World War II when unemployment started to rise.

Slayer

Slayer is an American metal band, formed in California. They appeared in 1981. They have released 12 albums, 6 music videos and a box set. They have received 5 Grammary Awards nominations.

Iron Maiden

Iron Maiden are a famous British heavy metal band. Formed in the 1970s and continued into the 21st century. The members of Iron Maiden are: Steve Harris, Dave Murray, Adrian Smith, Bruce Dickinson, Nicko McBrain and Janick Gers.

Metallica

Metallica is a heavy metal band formed in the United States. They were formed in LA in 1981. They grew rapidly with a growing fan base all over the country. They have won many awards, including 9 Grammy Awards.
